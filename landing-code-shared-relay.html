The difference

The work is shared. So is the steering.

Most agent tools make one person the driver and everyone else a spectator. Thinkpool keeps the pair inside the same decision loop.

Separate tools01

One person prompts. The other catches up.

  • Context gets copied between chats and calls.
  • The agent’s work happens behind one person’s screen.
  • Review begins after the decisions are already baked in.
Thinkpool room02

Two people shape the brief. The agent works in view.

  • One shared thread holds intent, constraints, and corrections.
  • Independent lanes stay visible to everyone in the room.
  • Either person can steer while the work is still moving.

The room

One room. Three distinct roles.

Neither person disappears when Pool starts working. The pair owns the direction. Pool carries the implementation.

Partner A

Names the intent.

Brings the problem, the context, and the outcome that actually matters.

Partner B

Tests the shape.

Challenges assumptions, adds constraints, and catches what one perspective misses.

Pool

Moves the work.

Turns the shared direction into visible progress and stays available for correction.
